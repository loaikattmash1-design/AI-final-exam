Ilia State University — GPA Calculation Rules

(Official rules, translated from Georgian.)

1. At Ilia State University, the GPA (Grade Point Average) is the sum of the products of each course's GP (Grade Point) and its earned credits, divided by the sum of the credits of all courses the student has taken:

GPA = Σ(course GP × course credits) ÷ Σ(course credits)

2. The GP (Grade Point) is the assessment received in a single course, converted onto a 4-point scale.

3. Grade categories

  • F — 0–50 points (0 points, Fail)
  • E — 51–60 points
  • D — 61–70 points
  • C — 71–80 points
  • B — 81–90 points
  • A — 91–95 points
  • A+ — 96–100 points

4. An E-category assessment (51–60) equals a fixed 1.00 GP. Assessments in the D, C, B and A categories (from 61 to 95 points inclusive) increase evenly, by 1/12 (≈0.08) per point. An A+ assessment (96–100 inclusive) is fixed at 4.00 GP.

5. A GPA is issued for every academic program except the doctoral level, and is not issued for certificate programs.

6. Only a final GPA is issued, shown in the diploma supplement (this does not apply to the one-step certified-physician program).

7. When calculating the final GPA, fractions are rounded to the nearest tenth.
